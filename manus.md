# Manus — Escape Velocity: Building our Future with an Agentic Way of Work
*Truls Erik Nielsen — Telenor × Google Cloud*

---

## Slide 1: Tittel

"Hello everyone. My name is Truls Erik Nielsen, and today I want to share the story of a fundamental choice we've made at Telenor B2B. It's the choice between continuing to optimize the existing system... or building a new one that can actually escape the gravitational pull of legacy."

---

## Slide 2: The Burning Platform

"But let's be honest about why we have to build it. Like many large, successful companies, we are standing on a burning platform.

The reality is that over 80% of our collective energy and resources are locked into just running the existing business. This leaves almost nothing left to build the future. It's a model that simply isn't sustainable in a world that is changing exponentially."

---

## Slide 3: Our Strategic Answer: Building an "Adaptive OS"

"Our way off this platform is to design and build a new operativsystem for the business. An Adaptive OS. A system designed for continuous learning and evolution, with a common language for humans and AI agents at its very core.

Business users give instructions in their own language. Agents execute across backends. Humans focus on decisions, relationships, and creativity."

---

## Slide 4: The Core Principle: A Common Language for Humans & Agents

"For this to work, humans and agents need that common language. Our method is simple in principle, but profound in practice: We treat all business knowledge as structured text.

This isn't a technical goal; it's a communication principle. In practice, it means this: an agent translates your natural language instruction into a structured change in a source file. A colleague reviews it. Git logs it. And every piece of organizational knowledge becomes auditable, searchable, and — crucially — something an agent can act on."

---

## Slide 5: Our First Proof in Production: The Analytics App

"Now, this is not just a theory. We have already built and launched the first application running on our new OS. This is the technical blueprint. The key is the semantic layer – the place where we define, once and for all, what 'churn' means, what 'revenue' includes, what an 'active customer' is. When that's done, the agent speaks the same language as the business. This is our proof, in full production."

---

## Slide 6: The Result: The "AI Analyst" in Action

"And this is the result: our AI Analyst, running in Looker. Its value is that it democratizes insight for everyone. You don't need to be a data scientist. You just need to be curious."

---

## Slide 7: The Unexpected Discovery: Creating Demand for Change

"But the most important result was one we didn't fully expect. I'll be honest – I thought this would be a much harder sell internally. We thought we would have to push this new way of working through the organization. Instead, the immediate success of the AI Analyst created a massive pull. It created a genuine hunger for this new methodology."

---

## Slide 8: What the Platform Unlocks

"And that hunger made us realize: the AI Analyst was just the first application. When an entire organization works this way — when 200 people all operate on the same structured foundation — a completely different set of things become possible.

Natural language queries directly against your full data warehouse. Shared scripts and models that work the same for everyone, everywhere. Quality standards automatically enforced across every document and process. And people who have never touched a terminal learning developer thinking — not through a course, but through daily work.

We're already measuring it: 75 FTE-equivalents of work absorbed by AI agents. 60x productivity on key workflows. Not someday — now.

This is what the platform unlocks. Not one tool. A new operating model."

---

## Slide 9: The True Potential: From Faster Answers to Impossible Insights

"But that success forced us to ask a much bigger question. What if an agent can do more than just give us faster answers? What if it can give us answers that were impossible to get before? What if it could see connections that no single human, or even team, could ever see on their own? Let's look at what that future looks like..."

---

## Slide 10: Scenario: This Is How It Works

"Let me show you a real example — something that happened in our own team.

Someone asks the agent: 'Make me a board presentation on the Security roadmap.'

The agent gets to work. It reads four source documents. It detects that a key product metric is outdated — and corrects it. Then it does something no human analyst would have thought to do: it identifies a brand-new opportunity that wasn't in any of the source documents — and adds it to the roadmap.

Fifteen minutes later, the presentation is built, the numbers are validated, the update is committed to Git, and all stakeholders are notified.

*(pause)*

No single person could have done that. Not in 15 minutes. Not without errors. Not with that level of cross-document awareness. No one even asked it to find the new opportunity — it just did. This is Content-as-Code in action. This is what the Adaptive OS makes possible."

The AI Analyst was the first, successful app. We are now building the next one for Sales Ops. But our North Star is to have the entire organization running on this new, adaptive OS."
